Information Security Management System (ISMS)

Information Security Management System (ISMS)

The term Information Security Management System (ISMS) is used in a generic manner. Organisations may choose to implement their ISMS, based on IS/ISO/IEC 27001, QCI CSMS Levels 1 to 3, or any other standard.

Entity-wide Planning

It is essential that governing bodies and top management of entities plan the use of a standard based approach with an entity-wide perspective. For example, in case it is planned to obtain ISMS certifications, this must be done at the enterprise level. Obtaining such certification for individual sites, without having an entity-wide plan, may not only be inefficient but also ineffective.

The governing bodies of entities have the responsibility to evaluate the requirement of one or more ISMSs to support the information security objectives of the entity. Each of the individual ISMSs, in this case, should be aligned with the entityâ€™s information security objectives, policies and processes and risk management.

The governing body should undertake the following with due diligence for the success of ISMS:

  • Mandatorily define and document an enterprise-level context for ISMS in their Organizations.

  • Approve the creation of ISMS.

  • Mandatorily define and document an enterprise-level ISMS objective and policy. It should provide directions to each ISMS implementation in the organisation to align it with the enterprise context.

  • All policy and regulatory directions received from and national nodal bodies and the regulators should be incorporated into the enterprise-level ISMS policy. This will help auditors assess whether all the policies have been incorporated.

  • Take decisions on acceptable levels of residual risk or appropriate risk treatments.

  • Provide each ISMS with communication channels and authority to inform interested parties and all persons in the scope of that ISMS.

Application of ISMS

CSEs and other organisations need to protect the confidentiality, integrity, and availability, of the information being processed as part of its business operations. The establishment of an effective ISMS will assist the organisation in meeting its information security risks in an appropriate manner and help reduce the organisation’s information security risks to an acceptable level.

Each ISMS implementation should ideally be implemented with a dedicated operation centre and must adhere to the following:

  • ISMS must implement controls for information security risk treatment in accordance with the policies approved by the governing board / top management of the CSE.

  • CSEs should describe the software applications and platforms that are used/ proposed to be used for managing the ISMS process (context and policy documentation, risk management, SoA mapping, evidence collection and management, compliance management etc). This will provide a common data repository for audit by the internal, external, and special audit teams.
